Actionable Insights for MRS v2

Recommendations

1. Leading Indicator Enhancement

Weight leading components higher: SKEW and ADL provide the earliest warnings. Consider increasing their weight in the composite score or creating a separate "early warning" sub-score.

2. Volume Integration

Add volume divergence indicator: Track 20-day volume trend vs price trend. When price makes new highs but volume is declining, flag as distribution warning. Consider adding a Volume Divergence Score (-0.5 when diverging).

3. Capitulation Detection

Add capitulation signal for bottom detection: When volume exceeds 2x the 20-day average AND VIX is in Stress state AND MRS is in RISK-OFF territory, flag as potential capitulation. This could improve recovery timing.

4. Regime Transition Alerts

Track regime transition velocity: Rapid transitions from RISK-ON to NEUTRAL (within 5-10 days) often precede larger drawdowns. Add an alert when regime changes faster than historical average.

5. Recovery Signal Enhancement

Add Buy-Side Signals: PC is the fastest component to recover at bottoms (7 days avg). Consider adding a "Recovery Signal" when PC turns positive while MRS is still in RISK-OFF territory. Combine with capitulation volume for higher confidence.

6. Threshold Optimization

Consider using MRS < -0.5 as primary warning: The 5-10% band shows the highest precision (43%). Using MILD RISK-OFF or worse as the warning level may reduce false positives while maintaining detection rate.

7. Bottom Detection Framework

Implement "Capitulation + Recovery" signal: Define a bottom signal as:

  • MRS in RISK-OFF territory (≤ -1.5)
  • Volume spike > 2x 20-day average
  • VIX in Stress state (Phi > 0.80)
  • Leading component (PC) turns positive
This multi-factor approach can help identify actionable bottoms.
